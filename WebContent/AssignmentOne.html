Assignment 1

The purpose of the pre-class homework is to get your dog into the right state of mind for training. In a nutshell, you need to get to a point where the dog has a strong desire for something that you have.

The easiest way to learn how to do this is to watch dogs play....especially a puppy trying to engage an older dog. You have to become the playful puppy and make your presence relevant to your dog. In normal dog school classes this is achieved thru delicious food treats. This is easy to do with a hungry, food motivated dog. Unfortunately it only lasts as long as you have food. I am not fond of spending my valuable time on training methods that don't hold up in the utility ring, so if you are looking for a class that teaches you how to train your dog with just food, let me know and I can find a trainer that teaches food training.

Instead of using food in our training routine, we will be using hands on play. We will blend in some food and some toys but the emphasis will be on physical play. Do two dogs use food and toys when they play with each other? Nope.

For the next couple of months your homework will be to put your hands on your dog and play with them. You will not be training any behaviors. Your goal is to learn how to have fun together without using food and toys.

IMPORTANT NOTE!!!! Do not do this assignment with dogs that were bred for protection: German Shepherds, Dobermans, Malinois, Pit Pulls. I do not know how to train dogs that were bred for protection work and you could get badly injured. Only do this kind of work with dogs that have NO history of biting and aggression. My only expertise is in training dogs like Golden Retrievers and Border Collies. If you have protection oriented dogs I can help you find an appropriate trainer but it is not me!

For this first assignment, make sure that your dog is quite hungry and well rested. If your dog is overweight, delay this first assignment until they have lost some weight. Consult your vet on how to lose weight. I recommend eating less and going on lots of walks but I am not a medical professional. Depending upon how active your dog is they might need to relax in a crate for 4 to 8 hours before you do this work. IOW, make sure your dog is at the correct weight, has not eaten for at least 8 hours and has been resting in a crate for at least 4 hours.

Take your dog outside and let them potty, then come back inside your house. Make sure all other dogs are crated and you are in a quiet room with no distractions.

Have a bowl of food close at hand. Sit on the floor with your dog and start to pet them roughly. Grab their collar and smile. Give them a piece of food about every 20 seconds. Crawl around the floor and roll around with them. Grab them all over their bodies. Touch their ears, their feet, their neck in a playful manner as you crawl all over the place. Just have fun! Be a puppy with them. This is a huge bonding experience. Let them chase your hands and feet. Push them around with your feet.

If your dog gets too excited, tone it down a bit and be less rough. If your dog bites you, say no and tap their nose. As soon as they calm down start to play again but at a lower level of arousal.

You can use a toy if you want to instead of the food or you can experiment with both. The goal is to get you both more comfortable with interacting physically. The goal is to teach your dog to play nicely and not get too rough with you.

If your dog is reluctant to play, then they might need to be hungrier or better rested. Some dogs just aren’t interested in this type of play or it might takes months of work to get them to enjoy it. If your dog is too rough and you can’t figure out how to stop them from biting you, do not continue! STOP!
